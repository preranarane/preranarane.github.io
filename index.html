Prerana Rane

As a Wireless Research Engineer at Intel's Next Generation Standards group, my work focused on research and standarization of 5G NR radio access technology. My contributions include research and development of PHY layer systems on features such as NR in unlicensed spectrum, Supporting NR operation from 52.6GHz to 71GHz, and NR Uplink Coverage Enhancements. I have actively participated in 3GPP 5G NR standardization efforts as a delegate on NR-Unlicensed and Non Terrestrial Networks topics. Several proposals on initial access, design of channel and synchronization raster, and power domain enhancement have been adopted in 3GPP 5G Standards.

Beyond the wireless domain, I've applied my skills to wearable biosensing devices, from designing DSP algorithms for blood perfusion sensor to implementing stress level monitoring in Smart Glasses. Outside of my technical pursuits, I engage actively with the IEEE Signal Processing Society and organize conferences that champion and celebrate women in tech. This breadth of experience positions me as a valuable asset in navigating the dynamic tech landscape.

As I seek the next chapter in my professional journey, I am actively exploring new opportunities. Explore my website to discover more about my research and projects. I'm always open to new collaborations and conversations. Let's connect and explore the possibilities together!
