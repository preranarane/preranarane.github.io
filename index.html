Prerana Rane

As a Wireless systems researcher with 3GPP RAN1 experience at Intel’s Next Generation Standards group, I worked on 5G NR PHY—initial access, uplink coverage enhancements, channel/synchronization raster design, and operation in the 57–71 GHz bands. I have actively participated in 3GPP 5G NR standardization efforts as a delegate, with several proposals adopted in 3GPP 5G Standards, 5+ granted U.S. patents.

Beyond cellular, wearables and biosensing—designing DSP algorithms for blood-perfusion sensing and implementing stress-monitoring features in smart glasses. I am actively involved with the IEEE Signal Processing Society as the Secretary of SCV Section and as a MMSP Reviewer.

I’m currently pursuing a Graduate Certificate in AI at Stanford, expanding into speech, audio, and multimodal ML. Recent projects include audio-visual deepfake detection, reinforcement-learning–based ASR, GAN-aided speaker recognition for low-resource settings, and audio-token compression for multimodal LLMs.

I’m open to collaborations and roles that combine wireless, signal processing, and machine learning.
